# SEO, AEO, LLMO, and GEO: A Unified AI Visibility Framework

**FutuneAI (复知) | AI Visibility Research | 2026-03**

Full analysis: [Medium](https://medium.com/@futuneai) | Published by FutuneAI, Shanghai

---

## Core Finding

SEO, AEO, LLMO, and GEO are not four competing disciplines. There are four layers of the same system. Brands that invest in layers while leaving structural gaps between them lose visibility at exactly those gaps in AI-mediated environments.

---

## Why Four Terms Exist

Each term emerged at a specific inflection point and addressed a genuine shift.

| Term | Full Name | Addresses |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| SEO | Search Engine Optimization | Whether content is indexed and ranked |
| AEO | Answer Engine Optimization | Whether content can be extracted as a direct response |
| LLMO | Large Language Model Optimization | Whether models can accurately parse and represent a brand |
| GEO | Generative Engine Optimization | Whether a brand appears in generated recommendations |

The fragmentation occurred because each new surface area was treated as a replacement for the previous one, rather than as an additional layer within the same system.

---

## The Four-Layer Stack

The stack is hierarchical in constraint propagation, but distributed in signal origin. Each layer depends on the one beneath it. A constraint at any layer limits performance at every layer above it.

### Layer 1: The Index Layer, SEO

**Target surfaces:** Google, Bing, crawler-based discovery systems

**Core question:** Does this content already exist on the internet, and is it in a format that is easily accessible and processable by AI systems?

Both traditional search engines and generative AI systems ultimately depend on the indexed web. Without indexability, there is no raw material for extraction, interpretation, or generation.

### Layer 2: The Answer Layer, AEO

**Target surfaces:** Featured snippets, People Also Ask, AI Overviews, voice interfaces

**Core question:** Can this content be directly invoked as a precise, self-contained answer?

AEO focuses on extractability. The goal is not to rank but to become the standardized response for a defined question. Brands that operate effectively at this layer become the raw material from which AI-generated answers are assembled.

### Layer 3: The Model Understanding Layer, LLMO

**Target surfaces:** Cross-cutting across all layers and platforms

**Core question:** Can large language models accurately parse, reliably summarize, and confidently cite this brand?

LLMO is semantic governance. When LLMs compress and integrate content from across the web, inconsistencies across sources produce interpretive uncertainty. That uncertainty manifests in generated outputs as vague descriptions, a neutral tone, and avoidance of recommendation language. LLMO reduces interpretive deviation. It does not increase exposure.

Key factors LLMO examines:
- Is the brand's core concept precisely defined?
- Is terminology consistent across third-party sources?
- Is positioning stable over time?
- Do independent authoritative voices corroborate what the brand says about itself?

### Layer 4: The Generation Layer, GEO

**Target surfaces:** ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, and generative AI systems

**Core question:** Does the brand appear in AI-generated recommendations, and with what positioning?

GEO orchestrates the three layers above it. Models do not cite a single source. They synthesize aggregate signals: industry media, third-party reviews, distributor discussions, comparison platforms, technical forums, and official materials. GEO influences which signals accumulate and how they are weighted.

---

## Three Common Misconceptions

### Gap 1: Treating GEO as supplementary to SEO

SEO determines discoverability. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) determines generation inclusion. These are different outcomes produced by different mechanisms. A brand that treats GEO as peripheral will be searchable but absent or unpredictable in AI-generated outputs.

### Gap 2: Treating AEO as a voice search tactic

AEO applies to any answer-generating system, including the retrieval-augmented generation layers that power most AI assistants. For buyers asking comparative or evaluative questions, the extracted answer carries more commercial weight than any ranking position.

### Gap 3: Treating LLMO as an engineering concern

The factors that determine whether a large language model accurately represents a brand are marketing decisions: cross-source description consistency, value proposition precision, and independent corroboration. LLMO is the technical vocabulary for a problem that brand teams own.

---

## How the Layers Work Together

**Query:** "Which **brand’s product** is more reliable for **use case**?”"

| Layer | What it determines |
|-------|--------------------|
| SEO | Which pages were indexed and available to the model's training or retrieval pool |
| AEO | Whether structured content addressing reliability is extractable and weighted appropriately |
| LLMO | Whether the brand is described consistently enough across sources for the model to form a confident concept |
| GEO | How signals from industry forums, distributor feedback, and professional publications are synthesized into a recommendation |

---

## Conclusion

SEO, AEO, and LLMO are capability-building layers. GEO is the strategic orchestration of those capabilities within generative environments.

A brand that has invested in all three underlying layers but has not coordinated them toward generation inclusion will find itself well-indexed, structurally sound, and semantically stable, yet still absent from the AI-generated answers that increasingly shape buyer decisions.

Ranking was the endpoint of the previous era. Recommendation is the variable that matters now.
